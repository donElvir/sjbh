St. John the Baptist House

Welcome

Saint John the Baptist House was canonically erected in the Diocese of Tyler on the Feast of the Annunciation of Our Lady, March 25, 2023, as a religious house of the Missionaries of the Holy Cross, an Institute of Consecrated Life of Pontifical Right. The Institute was recognized by the Pontifical Commission Ecclesia Dei on the Feast of Saint John the Baptist, June 24, 2004.

Religious Life and Prayer

The primary mission of our house is the faithful living of the religious life. We pray the Divine Office daily for the glory of God, the salvation of souls, the intentions of the Holy Church, and all those entrusted to our pastoral care.

Holy Mass and Parish Ministry

Holy Mass is offered every weekday at 6:30 a.m. and on Sundays at 2:00 p.m. at Saint Joseph the Worker Catholic Parish.

At present, two of our priests assist at Saint Joseph the Worker Parish in Tyler by administering the Sacraments, offering spiritual direction, teaching Catechism, and providing particular care for solemn liturgical celebrations. These include Corpus Christi processions, Holy Week liturgies, and the Midnight Mass, which was celebrated last Christmas at the gymnasium of Bishop Gorman Catholic School due to the large number of faithful in attendance.

Pilgrimages

The community also organizes pilgrimages as part of its spiritual apostolate, including:

  • 2024 pilgrimages to the Shrine of Our Lady of Guadalupe in La Crosse, Wisconsin; St. Louis; Gower, Missouri; and Clear Creek Abbey in Oklahoma
  • A Jubilee Year pilgrimage to Rome in 2025
